Ada Lovelace's Time Line

1815

Ada Byron is born December 10th to famous poet Lord Byron and Anne Isabella Byron.

1816

Ada's mother leaves Ada's father when Ada was 5 weeks old. A few months later, her father leaves England forever.

1824

Ada's father, Lord Byron passes away.

1828

Ada writes her first book at age 12. It is called Flyology, in this book she examines flight deeply.

1829

Ada was often sick in her childhood. A severe case of measles causes her to be bedridden. She suffered paralysis and could be seen using crutches in her teen years.

1833

Ada is introduced to Charles Babbage and his Difference Engine. Ada was very intrigued by this.

1835

Ada marries William King at age 19

1836

Ada welcomes her first child with William King, named Byron, after her father.

1837

Ada welcomes her second child with William King, named Anne, after her mother.

1838

William King becomes Earl of Lovelace, Ada became Countess of Lovelace.

1839

Ada welcomes her third child and final with William King, named Ralph Gordon

1841

Ada returns to work. She is tasked by Charles Babbage to translate a lecture into english. The lecture was about Babbage's Analytical Engine, based of his older Difference Engine.

1843

Ada finishes her translation, including detailed notes which ended up being more important than the translation itself. This work caused many to accredit Ada Lovelace as the world's first computer programmer.

1844

Ada struggles with addiction to a medicine her doctor had perscribed.

1848

Ada's eldest son leaves to go to sea at the age of 12.

1851

Ada is diagnosed with uterine cancer, at this point she was bedridden and extremely ill.

1852

Ada passes away on November 27.
